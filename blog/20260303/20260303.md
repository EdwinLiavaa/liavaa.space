## The Case Patrick Collins Made — and Why It Lands Differently in the Pacific

Patrick Collins, one of the smartest builders I know and the force behind Cyfrin, recently wrote about this VC migration with a line I keep returning to:

> *"The world's finance being solely powered by cryptocurrencies is inevitable."*

For someone sitting in New York or San Francisco, that might sound like bold idealism. For someone in the Pacific Islands, where families pay 10–15% to send money home, where banks are sparse and slow, where entire communities have been locked out of global finance by geography and legacy infrastructure, it does not sound like idealism at all. **It sounds like survival.**

Patrick points to the numbers i.e. the NYSE processes around $80 billion in daily transactions with roughly 12,000 employees. Uniswap does $2 billion a day with about 200. That is 60x the overhead reduction, and that's before you account for censorship resistance, 24/7 availability, and the radical accessibility of open financial rails.

For the Pacific, those numbers tell a story that is not abstract. They describe a future where a grandmother in Tonga can receive a remittance from her daughter in Auckland in seconds, for a fraction of a percent, not five to fifteen percent and without needing a branch that closes at 3pm, let alone the KYC and identity verification processes that exclude millions.

## What Pasifika Is Building

At **[Pasifika Web3 Tech Hub](https://pasifika.xyz/)**, we are not waiting for the world to figure out blockchain adoption. We are building it ourselves, for ourselves.

The Pasifika Data Chain is blockchain infrastructure built *by* Pacific Islanders, *for* Pacific Islanders. It is designed from the ground up with our communities' realities in mind:

- **Zero-gas transactions** on our native chain, removing the friction that excludes low income users
- **0.5% remittance fees** — a direct challenge to the predatory rates that drain Pacific family incomes
- **Data sovereignty** — keeping Pacific data under Pacific control, because our stories and our information belong to us
- **The PASI Token**, live on both the Pasifika Data Chain and Arbitrum One, bridging our community infrastructure with global DeFi liquidity

This is not a whitepaper promise. This is live infrastructure, deployed and operational, built by a community that has every reason to believe in what decentralized finance can do, because we have seen, firsthand, what its absence costs.

## The Moment We Are In

Patrick is right that some people will leave Web3 for AI. He is right not to hold it against them. But he is also right about what they may be leaving behind.

The "opportunities" of the last crypto cycle, the meme tokens, the liquidity mining schemes, the NFT speculation, those were noise. They obscured something real and important underneath. Now that the noise has cleared, what remains are the protocols with genuine utility i.e. Aave, Uniswap, stablecoins, zero knowledge privacy tools. Things that make financial lives meaningfully better.

The Pacific does not need the hype cycle. We need the utility. And the utility is here.

Smart contracts are cheaper. They are faster. They are fairer. And for communities that have been failed by expensive, slow, and exclusionary traditional finance, those three words are not a marketing pitch. They are a promise.
