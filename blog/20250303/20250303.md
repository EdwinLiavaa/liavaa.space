In the faltering democratic experiment known as Atlantis, political factions battled for supremacy beneath the swaying coconut palms of the Pacific. Hollow promises of representation echoed across the azure waters while the ruling elite clung to power through carefully crafted loopholes. Behind the ceremonial trappings of democratic institutions, the real governance happened through backroom deals and suspicious accounting, as reformers' well-intentioned but half-hearted attempts at true democracy repeatedly crashed against the reef of entrenched interests and political expediency.

Mary Lantis was not an Atlantis royalty. Her family had built a commercial empire over three generations, their name synonymous with progress and prosperity. "The Lantiss bring opportunity," the islanders would say, nodding with respect whenever a family member passed.

When Mary announced her plan to open "Bounty-For-Less," the kingdom's first international-style supermarket, people celebrated. Finally, a local business women can is able to supply affordable goods without the hefty markup that plagued the island's economy. The Commercial Bank of Atlantis approved her 20-million-dollar loan with unusual speed. The supermarket would stand proudly on the Royal Estate, a privilege few were granted.

"This will serve all Atlanteans," Mary declared at the groundbreaking ceremony, where both elites and commoners alike applauded. The King's representative smiled benevolently, handing her the royal permit.

Success bred ambition. Before Bounty-For-Less had even opened its doors, Mary secured another plot on the Royal Estate for a business complex. This time, she approached the Atlantis Development Bank, a government institution ostensibly created to fuel the kingdom's economic growth.

"Your vision aligns with our mission," said Anna Atla, the bank's CEO, a woman whose attire were always pressed and whose smile never quite reached her eyes.

Unbeknownst to her, Anna Atla, the ambitious and ruthless CEO of the Atlantis Development Bank, had her eyes on Mary's property. Anna had long coveted the waterfront for a luxury resort project that would line her pockets and those of her allies. Using her influence, Anna orchestrated a plan to pay off Mary's debt without her consent, effectively transferring the loan to the Development Bank.

Mary was blindsided when she received a notice stating her loan had been "settled" and her property was now under the control of the Development Bank. Desperate, she sought answers, but Anna's response was cold and calculated.

"Your debt has been resolved," Anna said with a smirk. "Consider it a favor. The Development Bank is now the rightful owner of the property, as per the terms of the loan agreement."

Mary protested, but the fine print in the loan documents buried under layers of legal jargon gave the bank the right to assume ownership if the debt was paid off by a third party. Anna had exploited this clause, using the bank's funds to settle Mary's debt and seize the property.

Three months later, Mary received a puzzling notice i.e. her original loan had been refinanced without her knowledge or consent. The Commercial Bank loan had vanished from her records, replaced by a new arrangement with the Development Bank. More curious still, an additional payment of six million dollars had been made from the Development Bank directly to the Royal Estate's accounts.

"There must be some mistake," Mary told her accountant, who merely shrugged, having seen stranger things in Atlantis.

When she confronted Atla, her explanation was a masterpiece of bureaucratic abstraction.

"The refinancing was a strategic consolidation initiative to optimize your investment portfolio while ensuring the sustainable development of royal properties," she said, managing to use many words while saying nothing at all.

Meanwhile, at the kingdom's only commercial port, another drama unfolded. The Ports Authority Board, comprised of four members had granted Mele's company exclusive stevedore rights. Other companies that had worked the docks for generations suddenly found themselves without livelihoods.

"The board determined that operational efficiency would be maximized through centralized management," explained the Ports Chairman, a man who had never set foot on a dock until his appointment.

Two competing stevedore companies filed lawsuits. Their court cessions are still in progress.

As whispers grew, the Prime Minister announced an audit, not of the transactions, but of "procedural compliance in maritime operations management.".

At exclusive dinner parties, island elites sipped imported champagne and spoke in riddles.

"Mary is certainly debt-free now," they would say with knowing smiles.

"And the Royal Estate is six million richer," came the inevitable reply.

"Some might call for transparency," ventured a visiting diplomat once.

The room fell silent before a group of elites laughed. "In Atlantis, my friend, the most transparent things are the waters surrounding us. Everything else... well, clarity can be so inconvenient."

Meanwhile, ordinary Atlanteans paid higher prices at the port, watched the Royal Estate expand with new construction projects, and wondered why progress always seemed to flow uphill, never reaching their villages below.

Mary herself had become an enigma, celebrated as a business pioneer in official narratives, yet whispered about in marketplaces. Was she victim or beneficiary? Pawn or player? The only certainty was that somewhere in the tangle of refinanced loans and royal permits, the line between enterprise and entitlement had blurred beyond recognition.

And as the sun set over Atlantis each evening, its golden light reflected off the windows of half-built developments, bad roads and government offices, creating the illusion that everything was, indeed, golden.

But upon the horizon, seismic activities were active with an anticipated volcanic eruption and gigantic tsunami waves that could destroy the entire continent in a matter of seconds.

# Ko e Tō 'a 'Atalanitisi - Ko ha Talanoa Nounou

'I he 'ahi'ahi fakapolitikale ki he fa'unga fakatemokalati ne 'iloa ko 'Atalanitisi, na'e fepakipaki 'a e ngaahi kulupu fakapolitikale ke ma'u 'a e mafai. Pea ko e ngaahi tala'ofa 'ikai mo'oni na'e 'ilonga 'i he funga oseni 'i he taimi na'e piki mai ai 'a e kau ma'u mafai makatu'unga 'i he ngaahi me'a kuo 'osi fa'u ka na'e 'ikai ke napangapangamalie. 'I he puipuitu'a 'o e ngaahi ouau ki he fa'unga temokalati 'ikai mateuteu, na'e iku hoko ai 'a e alea ki he ngaahi aleapau 'i ha ngaahi loki fakapulipuli pea iku leva kihe ngaahi fehalaaki fakapa'anga.

Na'e 'ikai ko ha hou'eiki 'o 'Atalanitisi 'a Mele Lanitisi. Ka na'e langa 'e hono famili ha makatu'unga fefakatau'aki 'i ha to'u tangata 'e tolu, pea ko honau hingoa na'e fehokotaki mo e fakalakalaka mo e tu'umālie. "Ko e Famili Lanitisi 'oku nau 'omai 'a e faingamālie," ko e pehē ia 'a e kakai 'o e 'otumotu, 'o faka'ilonga 'aki 'a e faka'apa'apa 'i he taimi 'oku 'alu atu ai ha mēmipa 'o e fāmili.

'I he taimi na'e fakahā ai 'e Mele 'ene palani ke fakaava 'a e "Bounty-For-Less," ko e 'uluaki falekoloa fakafonua 'o e pule'anga, na'e fiefia lahi 'a e kakai. Hilio ai, ko ha fefine pisinisi fakalotofonua 'e lava ke ne 'oatu 'a e koloa 'oku ma'ama'a ta'ekau ai 'a e totongi ma'olunga 'oku uesia ai 'a e 'ekonomika 'o e 'otumotu. Na'e tali 'e he Pangikē Fefakatau'aki 'a 'Atalanitisi 'ene no pa'anga 'e tola 'e 20 miliona pea ko e falekoloa fakafonua 'e tu'u he tofi'a 'o e Tu'i, ko ha faingamālie na'e 'oange ki ha ni'ihi tokosi'i pe.

"'E tokoni eni ki he kakai 'Atalanitisi kotoa," na'e fakahā 'e Mele 'i he kātoanga huufi, 'a ia na'e fakatou paasipasi ai 'a e kau ma'olunga mo e kakai angamaheni. Na'e malimali fiefia 'a e fakafofonga 'o e Tu'i, 'o 'oange kiate ia 'a e ngofua fakatu'i.

Ko e lavame'a na'e fakatupu 'amanaki. Kimu'a pea fakaava 'a e matapā 'o e Bounty-For-Less, na'e toe ma'u 'e Mele ha konga kelekele 'i he tofi'a 'o e Tu'i ki ha fa'unga pisinisi 'e taha. 'I he taimi ko 'eni, na'e ne 'alu ki he Pangikē Fakalakalaka 'a 'Atalanitisi, ko ha fa'unga 'a e pule'anga na'a ne fakatupu 'a e tupulaki faka'ekonomika 'o e pule'anga.

"Ko ho'o visone 'oku fenāpasi mo 'emau taumu'a," na'e pehē 'e 'Ana 'Atala, ko e pule lahi 'o e pangikē, ko ha fefine na'e vala lelei ma'u pē pea ko 'ene malimali na'e kaiha'asi pe mei hono fofonga.

Fakafokifā kiate ia, ko 'Ana 'Atala, ko e pule lahi 'o e pangikē na'a ne siofi 'a e kelekele 'o Mele. Kuo fuoloa 'a e maanumanu 'a 'Ana ki he matātahi ki ha ngāue ki ha nofo'anga fakatalifononga te ne tānaki 'a e pa'anga mo kinautolu 'oku nau kaungame'a. 'O ngāue'aki 'a 'ene takiekina, na'e fa'u 'e 'Ana ha palani ke totongi kotoa 'a e mo'ua 'o Mele ta'e ma'u 'a 'ene loto fiemālie kiai, pea hoko ai e liliu 'o e no meia Mele ki he Pangikē Fakalakalaka.

Na'e 'ohovale 'a Mele 'i he 'ene ma'u 'a e fanongonongo na'e "Totongi" 'a 'ene no pea ko hono kelekele kuo 'i he malumalu ia 'o e Pangikē Fakalakalaka. 'I he'ene loto-hoha'a, na'a ne kumi ha ngaahi tali, ka ko e tali 'a 'Ana na'e momoko pe mo fakapoto.

"Kuo totongi ho mo'ua," ko e pehē ange ia 'a 'Ana mo 'ene malimali. "Lau ia ko ha tokoni. Ko e Pangikē Fakalakalaka ko e mamafi totonu ia 'o e kelekele, 'o fakatatau ki he ngaahi tu'utu'uni 'o e aleapau no."

Na'e fakafepaki 'a Mele, ka ko e fakamatala fakalao 'i he aleapau no na'e tanu 'i lalo 'i he ngaahi kupu'i lea fakalao na'e 'oange ki he pangikē 'a e totonu kenau ma'u 'a e e mafai kapau 'e totongi 'a e mo'ua. Na'e faka'aonga'i 'e 'Ana 'a e kupu ko 'eni, 'o ngāue'aki 'a e pa'anga 'a e pangikē ke totongi 'a e mo'ua 'o Mele kanau puke 'a e kelekele.

Hili 'a e māhina 'e tolu, na'e ma'u 'e Mele ha fanongonongo 'ikai mahino, 'a ia ko 'ene no kuo 'osi totongi ta'e 'i ai ha'ane 'ilo pe loto ki ai. Na'e pulia 'a e leekoti no 'a e Pangikē Fefakatau'aki mei he'ene ngaahi lekooti, 'o fetongi'aki ha fokotu'utu'u fo'ou mo e Pangikē Fakalakalaka. Pea koe me'a 'oku toe fakatotolo, ko ha totongi 'e tola 'e ono miliona kuo fakahoko mei he Pangikē Fakalakalaka fakahangatonu ki he ngaahi 'akauni 'o e Tofi'a 'o e Tu'i.

"Kuo mahino pau mai 'oku 'i ai e fehālaaki heni," ko e pehē ange ia 'a Mele ki he'ene 'akauiniteni, 'a ia na'a ne kamokamo pē, he kuo ne 'osi mamata ha ngaahi me'a 'oku toe fakaofo ange 'i 'Atalanitisi.

'I he taimi na'a ne fehangahangai ai mo 'Atala, na'e fakamatala pe 'a Atala ko e founga pe eni he fa'unga fakapule'anga.

"Ko hono toe fakapa'anga eni ki ha ngāue fakalakalaka ke totongi ho'o pōtifōliō 'inivesimeni lolotonga hono fakapapau'i 'a e fakalakalaka 'oku tolonga mo tu'uloa ki he ngaahi tofi'a kelekele fakatu'i".

'I he vaha'a taimi ko 'eni, 'i he taulanga fakakomesiale pē 'e taha 'o e pule'anga, na'e hoko ha toe me'a fakaofo ia ai 'e taha. Ko e Poate 'o e Mafai 'o e Ngaahi Taulanga, 'a ia na'e kau ai 'a e mēmipa 'e fā, kuo nau foaki kia Mele 'a e totonu fakataautaha ke tauhi 'a e taulanga. Ko e ngaahi kautaha kehe kuo nau ngāue 'i he taulanga ki he ngaahi to'u tangata, fakafokifā pe kuo 'ikai toe 'i ai ha'anau ngaue kenau ma'u mo'ui mei ai.

"Na'e fakapapau'i 'e he poate ko e ngāue 'e toe lelei ange 'i he pule'i pe 'e ha tokotaha," na'e fakamatala'i 'e he Sea 'o e Ma'u Mafai 'o e Ngaahi Taulanga, ko ha tangata na'e te'eki ke ne tu'u 'i ha taulanga ki mu'a 'i hono fakanofo.

Na'e faile leva 'e ha ongo kautaha ha faka'ilo fakalo pea 'oku kei lele 'ae hopo koia 'i he fakamaau'anga.

Ka 'oku 'i ai 'a e ngaahi fefanafanahi, na'e fakahā 'e he Palemia ha 'ātita, 'ikai ko e ngaahi ngāue fefakatau'aki pe, ka ko e "talangofua ki he ngaahi founga 'i hono pule'i 'o e ngaahi ngāue 'i tahi."

'I he ngaahi ma'u me'atokoni efiafi fakafonua mahu'inga, ko e kau ma'olunga 'o e 'otumotu na'a nau inu sēmipeini mo lea 'aki he'enau ngaahi talanoa.

"Ko 'osi e mo'ua ia 'o Mele," Ko 'enau lea ia mo e malimali.

"Pea kuo 'osi totongi moe ono miliona ia ki he Tofi'a 'o e Tu'i," Ko e tali mai ia 'a e tokotaha na'e ta'e'amanekina.

"'Oku tonu nai ke ui ha ni'ihi 'o faka'eke'eke ke 'i ai ha 'ata ki tu'a?," na'e fakafehu'i 'e ha tokotaha fakafofonga mei muli.

Na'e fakalongo 'a e matanga kimu'a pea kata ha kulupu 'o e kau ma'olunga. "'I 'Atalanitisi ni, ko e ngaahi me'a pe 'oku 'ata ki tu'a 'a e ngaahi fukahitahi 'oku ne takatakai kitautolu."

'I he vaha'a taimi ko 'eni, na'e totongi 'e he kau 'Atalanitisi angamaheni 'a e ngaahi totongi ma'olunga ange 'i he taulanga, mamata ki he tofi'a 'o e Tu'i 'oku fakatupulekina 'aki 'a e ngaahi polōseki langa fo'ou, mo fifili pe ko e hā 'oku pehee ai 'a e fakalakalaka,  'o tafe ma'u pē ki 'olunga, 'o 'ikai a'u ki honau ngaahi kolo 'i lalo.

Ko e tu'unga na'e 'i ai a Mele na'e hoko ko ha me'a 'ikai mahino, na'e fakamālō 'ia 'o hangē ha takimu'a pisinisi 'i he ngaahi talanoa 'e ni'ihi, ka 'oku kei fefanafana'i pe hono hingoa 'i he māketi. Ko e tokotaha na'e kākā'i pe ko ia na'a ne ma'u 'a e lelei? Ko e me'a pau pē ke ai kiai e sio ki he ngaahi nō kuo toe fakapa'anga mo e ngaahi ngofua ki he tofi'a fakatu'i, he ko e laine 'i he vaha'a 'o e ngāue mo e totonu kuo 'osi 'ikai ko toe 'iloa.

Pea 'i he taimi 'oku tō hifo ai 'a e la'ā 'i 'Atalanitisi 'i he efiafi kotoa pē, ko hono ngaahi huelo koula na'e 'asi mai he ngaahi matapā sio'ata 'o e ngaahi langa kuo kamata, ngaahi hala luoluo mo kovi mo e ngaahi 'ōfisi fakapule'anga, 'o ne fakatupu 'a e fakakaukau, ko e me'a kotoa pē, ko e mo'oni? na'e koula?

Ka 'i he too 'anga 'o e la'ā, na'e longomo'ui mai ai e ngāue 'a natula ki he mo'unga afi, mofuike moe 'amanaki ki ha ngaahi peau kula lalahi 'e lava ke ne faka'auha 'a e kotoa 'o e fonua 'i ha ngaahi lau sekoni pē.
